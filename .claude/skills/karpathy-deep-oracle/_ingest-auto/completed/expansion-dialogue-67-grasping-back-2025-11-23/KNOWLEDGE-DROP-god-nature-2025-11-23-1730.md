## Summary

## Key Content

### Core Concepts Covered
1. **Primordial Nature** - Eternal ordering of eternal objects (possibilities)
   - Grades eternal objects by mutual relevance
   - Provides relevance for particular occasions
   - Gives initial aims to concrescent occasions

2. **Consequent Nature** - God's reception of actual world
   - Prehends every actual occasion (nothing lost)
   - Integrates into unified satisfaction
   - "Tender care that nothing be lost"

3. **Superjective Nature** - God's influence passing back
   - Satisfaction qualifying future creativity
   - "Particular providence for particular occasions"

4. **Why God is Needed**
   - Problem of relevance (how possibilities become relevant)
   - Problem of initial aims (where do they come from?)
   - Problem of value preservation (objective immortality)
   - Problem of order AND novelty

5. **Non-Anthropomorphic Conception**
   - Not imperial ruler but poet/companion
   - Persuasion not coercion
   - Beyond omnipotence fallacy

6. **Mutual Immanence**
   - Whitehead's antitheses (PR 348)
   - God creates World / World creates God
   - Panentheism not pantheism
