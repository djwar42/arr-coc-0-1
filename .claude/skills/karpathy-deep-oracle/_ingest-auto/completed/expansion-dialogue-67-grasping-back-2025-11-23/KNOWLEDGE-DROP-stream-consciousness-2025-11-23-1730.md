## Summary

Created comprehensive knowledge file on William James' stream of consciousness concept from *The Principles of Psychology* (1890). The file covers James' revolutionary characterization of consciousness as a continuous flow rather than discrete mental atoms, with extensive analysis of the fringe/halo concept, substantive vs transitive parts, temporal continuity, and selective attention.

---

## Key Concepts Covered

### Core Ideas
1. **Stream Metaphor**: Consciousness flows like a river, not a chain of discrete ideas
2. **Four Characters**: Personal ownership, constant change, sensible continuity, selective interest
3. **Substantive & Transitive Parts**: Resting places vs flights in the stream
4. **Fringes/Halos**: Vague feelings of relation surrounding focal content
5. **Temporal Continuity**: Unity across time-gaps and qualitative changes
6. **Selective Attention**: Consciousness always choosing, always interested

### Primary Sources Used
- James (1892) "The Stream of Consciousness" - full text from psychclassics.yorku.ca
- Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Temporal Consciousness entry (partial)

### Key Quotes Captured
- "Consciousness does not appear to itself chopped up in bits... it flows"
- "A feeling of *and*, a feeling of *if*, a feeling of *but*"
- "The free water of consciousness that psychologists resolutely overlook"
- "Like a bird's life, it seems to be an alternation of flights and perchings"
