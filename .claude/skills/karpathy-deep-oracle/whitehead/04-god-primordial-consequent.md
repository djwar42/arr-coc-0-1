# Whitehead's God: Primordial and Consequent Natures

## Overview

In Alfred North Whitehead's process philosophy, God is conceived as a dipolar actual entity with two inseparable but distinguishable natures: the **primordial nature** and the **consequent nature**. This conception represents one of the most significant departures from classical theism in Western philosophy, offering a God who is genuinely affected by the world while providing the ground for all possibility and order.

The dipolar God is essential to Whitehead's metaphysical system - without God there would be no order or novelty and hence no world. Yet Whitehead insists that God is not an exception to metaphysical categories but their chief exemplification.

---

## Section 1: The Primordial Nature - Ordering of Eternal Objects

### Definition and Character

The primordial nature of God is "the unconditioned conceptual valuation of the entire multiplicity of eternal objects" (Process and Reality, p. 46). This represents God's eternal envisagement of all pure possibilities - what Leibniz called knowledge of all possible worlds.

**Key characteristics:**
- **Conceptual**: Pure conceptual feelings without physical prehensions
- **Eternal/Non-temporal**: Not defined by reference to any particular historical event
- **Unchanging**: God's primordial conceptual nature is unchanged by reason of its final completeness
- **Deficient in actuality**: By itself, it is abstract and requires the consequent nature for full actuality

### Three Primary Functions

**1. Grading Eternal Objects by Mutual Relevance**

The primordial nature grades eternal objects in terms of their relevance to one another:

> "The general relationships of eternal objects to each other, relationships of diversity and of pattern, are their relationships in God's conceptual realization. Apart from this realization there is mere isolation indistinguishable from nonentity." (PR 392)

This provides the **metaphysical stability** of the universe - the coherence of possibility space itself.

**2. Grading Relevance for Particular Occasions**

> "By reason of the actuality of this primordial valuation of pure potentials, each eternal object has a definite, effective relevance to each concrescent process." (PR 64)

This ordering makes possible both **order and novelty** in the world - determining which possibilities are relevant for which actual occasions.

**3. Providing Initial Aims**

The primordial nature provides the **initial aim** for each concrescent occasion:

> "God is the principle of concretion; namely he is that actual entity from which each temporal concrescence receives that initial aim from which its self-causation starts." (PR 374)

The initial aim:
- Determines initial gradations of relevance
- Constitutes the autonomous subject in its primary phase
- Provides initial conceptual valuations
- Guides toward maximal actualization

### The Realm of Pure Potentiality

The primordial nature is "the unlimited conceptual realization of the absolute wealth of potentiality" (PR 521). It represents:
- **General potentiality**: All mutually consistent or alternative possibilities
- **Logical space**: The infinite ways eternal objects may be related
- **The ground of relevance**: Why some possibilities matter for some occasions

**Critical insight**: Without this primordial ordering, eternal objects would be "mere isolation indistinguishable from nonentity" - pure chaos without the possibility of ingression into actuality.

## Section 2: The Consequent Nature - Growth from Actualities

### Definition and Character

The consequent nature of God is "the physical prehension by God of the actualities of the evolving universe" (PR 134). It represents God's reception and integration of the actual world.

**Key characteristics:**
- **Physical**: Involves physical feelings of actual occasions
- **Temporal**: Grows with the world, consequent upon creaturely decisions
- **Derivative**: Derived from objectification of the world in God
- **Completing**: Provides the fullness that the abstract primordial nature lacks

### Two Primary Functions

**1. Complete Prehension of All Actualities**

God prehends every component of the satisfaction of every actual occasion - nothing in finite actuality is excluded:

> "A tender care that nothing be lost" (PR 525)

This is **objective immortality** - the permanent preservation of all achieved value in the divine memory.

**2. Integration into Unity**

The consequent nature weaves all physical feelings into one unity:

> "The consequent nature of God is the realization of the actual world in the unity of his nature, and through the transformation of his wisdom, his completed synthesis of physical feeling." (PR 524)

> "The consequent nature of God is the fulfillment of his experience by his reception of the multiple freedom of actuality into the harmony of his own actualization." (PR 530)

This integration requires the **supplemental phases** - not just conformal feelings but the mental pole's integrative activity.

### The Weaving Process

The consequent nature performs a cosmic weaving:
- **Reception**: Takes in all worldly occasions
- **Harmonization**: Integrates conflicting data that finite occasions cannot
- **Preservation**: Nothing is lost - all value preserved
- **Transformation**: Through divine wisdom, discord becomes material for new harmony

**Critical insight**: God's capacity for harmonization is made possible by the unlimited conceptual resources of the primordial nature - the boundless realm of eternal objects available for integration.

## Section 3: Why God is Needed in Process Philosophy

### The Problem of Relevance

Without God, Whitehead's system faces a critical problem: How do eternal objects (pure possibilities) become relevant to actual occasions?

**The solution**: God's primordial nature provides the ordering that makes some possibilities relevant for some occasions. Without this:
- Eternal objects would be chaotic multiplicity
- No basis for cosmic order
- No ground for novelty - no "lure" toward new possibilities

### The Problem of Initial Aims

Every actual occasion needs an initial aim to begin its process of concrescence. Where does this come from?

**The solution**: God provides initial aims customized for each nascent occasion:
- Derived from the primordial valuation
- Tailored to the specific situation (requiring consequent nature's knowledge)
- Urging toward the best possible outcome

### The Problem of Value Preservation

Without objective immortality in God, all achieved value would simply perish:

**The solution**: The consequent nature preserves all value:
- Perfect divine memory
- Nothing lost
- The universe's achievements matter eternally

### The Problem of Order and Novelty

How can a universe have both stable order and genuine novelty?

**The solution**: The primordial nature provides:
- Eternal ordering for stability
- Graded relevance for novelty
- Initial aims urging toward new possibilities

## Section 4: Non-Anthropomorphic Conception

### Beyond the Imperial God

Whitehead explicitly rejects anthropomorphic conceptions that fashion God in the image of imperial rulers:

> "The Church gave unto God the attributes which belonged exclusively to Caesar." (PR 342)

Instead, God is conceived as:
- **The poet of the world**: Leading with vision of truth, beauty, goodness
- **The great companion**: Fellow-sufferer who understands
- **The principle of concretion**: Making determinate what was indeterminate

### Not a Supernatural Intervention

God does not "intervene" in nature because God is always already involved:
- God is not outside the metaphysical categories
- Divine action is through persuasion, not coercion
- God provides initial aims, not final determinations

### Persuasion Rather Than Coercion

> "One of the greatest intellectual discoveries in the history of religion" - Whitehead on Plato's insight

God acts by:
- **Luring**: Presenting ideal possibilities
- **Persuading**: Urging toward maximal value
- **Respecting**: Honoring creaturely self-determination

God does NOT act by:
- Unilateral determination
- Overriding creaturely freedom
- Coercive force

### Beyond Omnipotence Fallacy

The "omnipotence fallacy" assumes that if a state of affairs is logically possible, an omnipotent being could single-handedly bring it about.

**Process response**: Some logically possible states require multiple freedoms:
- Contracts require two parties
- Love requires free response
- "It takes two to tango"

## Section 5: Mutual Immanence - God and World

### Whitehead's Antitheses

The closing pages of Process and Reality present paired statements showing mutual immanence:

> "It is as true to say that God is permanent and the World fluent, as that the World is permanent and God is fluent.
>
> It is as true to say that God is one and the World many, as that the World is one and God many.
>
> It is as true to say that, in comparison with the World, God is actual eminently, as that, in comparison with God, the World is actual eminently.
>
> It is as true to say that the World is immanent in God, as that God is immanent in the World.
>
> It is as true to say that God transcends the World, as that the World transcends God.
>
> It is as true to say that God creates the World, as that the World creates God." (PR 348)

### Understanding the Antitheses

**God permanent / World fluent**:
- God's primordial nature eternally envisions all possibility
- World of actual occasions comes and goes

**World permanent / God fluent**:
- World is permanent as objectively immortal in God's memory
- God's consequent nature constantly grows with new experience

**God creates World / World creates God**:
- God creates by providing initial aims and possibilities
- World creates in God by contributing to divine experience

### Not Pantheism

Whitehead's view is panentheism, not pantheism:
- **Pantheism**: World = God (identity)
- **Panentheism**: World IN God (inclusion)

The world is within God but God transcends the world. The world contributes to God's experience but does not exhaust the divine reality.

## Section 6: The Superjective Nature

### Definition

Whitehead briefly mentions a third aspect - the "superjective nature":

> "The 'superjective' nature of God is the character of the pragmatic value of his specific satisfaction qualifying the transcendent creativity in the various temporal instances." (PR 135)

### Function

The superjective nature represents God's **objective immortality** - God's influence passing back into the world:

> "The perfected actuality passes back into the temporal world, and qualifies this world so that each temporal actuality includes it as an immediate fact of relevant experience." (PR 532)

This is "the particular providence for particular occasions."

### Relationship to Other Natures

The superjective nature is not a separate part but represents:
- The objective side of combined primordial and consequent functions
- How God conditions the world as a result of prehension and harmonization
- God's satisfaction qualifying future creativity

**Critical insight**: There is only ONE satisfaction for God as actual entity, containing all prehensions. The superjective nature is the objective immortality of God as a whole.

## Section 7: Key Passages from Process and Reality

### On the Primordial Nature

> "The primordial nature is conceptual, the consequent nature is the weaving of God's physical feelings upon his primordial concepts." (PR 524)

> "God's conceptual nature is unchanged, by reason of its final completeness. But his derivative nature is consequent upon the creative advance of the world." (PR 523)

> "This is the conception of God, according to which he is considered as the outcome of creativity, as the foundation of order, and as the goad towards novelty." (PR 135)

### On the Consequent Nature

> "The consequent nature of God is conscious; and it is the realization of the actual world in the unity of his nature, and through the transformation of his wisdom." (PR 524)

> "God's conceptual nature is the same for all; but he is not before all creation, but with all creation." (PR 521)

### On Divine Influence

> "He is the poet of the world, with tender patience leading it by his vision of truth, beauty, and goodness." (PR 346)

### On the Unity of the Two Natures

> "Thus, analogously to all actual entities, the nature of God is dipolar. He has a primordial nature and a consequent nature." (PR 524)

The natures cannot be separated:
- Primordial without consequent = deficient in actuality
- Consequent without primordial = no basis for harmonization

Whitehead's dipolar God with primordial and consequent natures offers a profound reconception of the divine:

1. **Primordial nature**: Eternal ordering of all possibilities - makes relevance possible
2. **Consequent nature**: Growing with the world - receives and integrates all actuality
3. **Superjective nature**: God's influence passing back into the world

This is not the unmoved mover of classical theism but:
- The poet leading with vision
- The great companion who suffers with us
- The ground of both order and novelty

For ARR-COC-0-1, this provides a model of relevance realization that is:
- **Grounded**: In primordial ordering of possibilities
- **Dynamic**: Growing with processed content
- **Persuasive**: Guiding but not determining
- **Integrative**: Weaving multiplicity into unity

The process God performs at cosmic scale what relevance realization performs at cognitive scale - ordering the infinite into the determinate through prehension, valuation, and integration.
