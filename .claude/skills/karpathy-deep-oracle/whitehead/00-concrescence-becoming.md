# Whitehead: Concrescence and Becoming

## Overview

Alfred North Whitehead's (1861-1947) process philosophy represents one of the most comprehensive and radical departures from traditional Western metaphysics. At its core is the concept of **concrescence**--the process by which "the many become one and are increased by one." This document explores Whitehead's foundational concepts of concrescence, actual occasions, and the primacy of becoming over being.

From [Process and Reality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Process_and_Reality) (Whitehead 1929):
> "An actual entity is concrete because it is such a particular concrescence of the universe."

---

## Section 1: Concrescence Defined

### The Many Become One

**Concrescence** is Whitehead's technical term for the process by which an actual occasion comes into being through the integration of various influences from its past into a single, unified moment of experience.

From [Open Horizons - Whitehead's Creative Use of Concrescence](https://www.openhorizons.org/from-old-words-to-new-meanings-whiteheads-creative-use-of-concrescence.html) (accessed 2025-11-23):

The word "concrescence" originally referred to:
- **Physical coming together** or solidification (1610-1800s)
- **Biological coalescence** of cells, organs, or organisms (1878-1890s)
- **Embryological growth** of parts during development (1890-1956)
- **Botanical union** of cell walls (1900)

Whitehead transformed this concept to capture:
- **Subjective unification** of many influences from the past
- **Active integration** where the subject shapes elements according to purpose
- **Dynamic creation** that is both continuation of past and expression of novelty

### The Oscillation Between One and Many

From [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Process Philosophy](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/process-philosophy/) (accessed 2025-11-23):

> "The basic unit of reality in Whitehead's system is an event-like entity called 'actual occasion,' which is the procedural integration or 'concrescence' of processes of data transfer ('prehensions') into unities that become new data."

The fundamental rhythm of reality:
1. **Many** past occasions exist as data
2. **Concrescence** integrates them into **one** new occasion
3. This **one** becomes datum for future **many**
4. Cycle repeats eternally

This "creative advance" is the heartbeat of Whitehead's cosmos.

## Section 2: Actual Occasions as Events of Becoming

### The Final Real Things

From [Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Process Philosophy](https://iep.utm.edu/processp/) (accessed 2025-11-23):

> "These events, called actual entities or actual occasions, are 'the final real things of which the world is made up' (Whitehead, Process and Reality, 18). They occur very briefly and are characterized by the power of self-determination and subjective immediacy."

**Key characteristics of actual occasions:**
- **Momentary**: They occur briefly, then "perish"
- **Self-determining**: Each occasion decides how to integrate its data
- **Experiential**: All occasions have some form of experience (not necessarily conscious)
- **Internally related**: Connected to their entire past world
- **Unique**: No two occasions are identical

### Not Particles, Not Substances

Actual occasions are NOT:
- Material particles (they are events, not things)
- Substances (they don't endure unchanged)
- Instantaneous points (they have temporal thickness)
- Passive recipients (they actively create themselves)

They ARE:
- Drops of experience
- Moments of creative synthesis
- Self-creating subjects that become objects for others
- The "atoms" of reality in a processual sense

## Section 3: Why Occasions, Not Substances

### The Problem with Substance Metaphysics

Traditional Western metaphysics (following Aristotle) held that:
- Reality consists of **substances** (unchanging bearers of properties)
- Change is **secondary** to being
- Properties are **externally** related to substances
- Time is a **container** for unchanging things

Whitehead rejected this because:

From Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy:
> "Substance metaphysics proceeds from the intuition--first formulated by the pre-Socratic Greek philosopher Parmenides--that being should be thought of as simple, hence as internally undifferentiated and unchangeable."

This leads to insoluble problems:
1. **Mind-body dualism** (how do static substances interact?)
2. **Problem of change** (how can the unchanging change?)
3. **External relations** (how do independent things relate?)
4. **Zeno's paradoxes** (how does static being move?)

### The Process Alternative

Whitehead's solution:
- Reality consists of **occasions** (self-creating events)
- Becoming is **primary** to being
- Relations are **internal** (constitutive of entities)
- Time is **constituted by** the succession of occasions

From IEP:
> "Where substance metaphysics and modern science have posited that the world is made up of material objects, Whitehead argues that 'organism' is a better term for things that exist."

Organisms are:
- **Interdependent** (not self-sustaining)
- **Internally related** (not externally connected)
- **Value-laden** (not valueless)
- **Active** (not passive)
- **Intrinsically self-moving** (not requiring external force)

## Section 4: Process vs Substance Metaphysics

### Fundamental Differences

| Aspect | Substance Metaphysics | Process Metaphysics |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Basic entities | Static substances | Dynamic occasions |
| Time | Container for being | Constituted by becoming |
| Relations | External | Internal |
| Change | Secondary, derivative | Primary, fundamental |
| Identity | Numerical identity over time | Pattern persistence |
| Causation | Efficient cause | Creative synthesis |
| Experience | Emergent property | Fundamental feature |

### The Bifurcation of Nature

Whitehead criticized the "bifurcation of nature" into:
- Primary qualities (measurable, objective)
- Secondary qualities (experiential, subjective)

This creates an unbridgeable gap between:
- The world as science describes it
- The world as we experience it

Process philosophy heals this bifurcation by making experience fundamental to all actuality, not just human minds.

## Section 5: Becoming Over Being

### The Primacy of Process

From Stanford Encyclopedia:
> "Process philosophy is based on the premise that being is dynamic and that the dynamic nature of being should be the primary focus of any comprehensive philosophical account of reality."

Heraclitus (born ca. 560 BCE) first articulated this:
- "This world-order... is... an ever living fire"
- All observable changes result from "strife" of opposing forces
- Yet dynamicity is tied to balance and "measure"

Whitehead developed this into a comprehensive metaphysics where:
- **Being = Becoming**: To be is to become
- **Perishing = Objectification**: Death enables new life
- **Actuality = Activity**: Real things are happenings

### The Creative Advance

Reality is a "creative advance into novelty":
1. Past occasions provide data (settled facts)
2. Present occasion synthesizes data (creative decision)
3. Novel form emerges (genuine newness)
4. New occasion perishes, becomes data (objective immortality)

This ensures that:
- The past is never lost (preserved in all futures)
- The future is never predetermined (creativity is real)
- Novelty is genuinely possible (not just rearrangement)

## Section 6: Key Passages from Process and Reality

### Fundamental Definitions

From Process and Reality (1929):

On actual entities:
> "Actual entities--also termed 'actual occasions'--are the final real things of which the world is made up. There is no going behind actual entities to find anything more real." (PR 18)

On concrescence:
> "'Concrescence' is the name for the process in which the universe of many things acquires an individual unity." (PR 21)

On creativity:
> "The many become one, and are increased by one." (PR 21)

On becoming:
> "How an actual entity becomes constitutes what that actual entity is... Its 'being' is constituted by its 'becoming.' This is the 'principle of process.'" (PR 23)

### The Subject-Superject

An actual occasion is both:
- **Subject**: The experiencing entity during concrescence
- **Superject**: The completed datum for future occasions

This dual nature captures how:
- Private experience becomes public fact
- Subjectivity creates objectivity
- Process yields product

## Section 7: Contemporary Explanations

### Modern Interpretations

From [Footnotes2Plato - Why the World is Unfinished](https://footnotes2plato.com/2024/12/17/why-the-world-is-unfinished-whitehead-in-20-minutes/) (accessed 2025-11-23):

> "There is no continuity of becoming, despite the extensive continuity of the physical universe; rather, there is a becoming of continuity."

This means:
- The world appears continuous to us
- But actually consists of discrete occasions
- Continuity is an achievement, not a given
- Each moment recreates the apparent continuum

### Relation to Quantum Physics

Whitehead's occasions share features with quantum events:
- **Discreteness**: Quantized, not continuous
- **Indeterminacy**: Not fully determined by past
- **Holism**: Internally related to whole universe
- **Observer-participation**: Measurement affects outcome

From Stanford Encyclopedia:
> "Quantum physics brought on the dematerialization of physical matter--matter in the small could no longer be conceptualized as a Rutherfordian planetary system of particle-like objects."

### Relation to Biology

Process philosophy provides better descriptions for:
- **Metabolism**: Continuous self-transformation
- **Development**: Emergence of novel forms
- **Evolution**: Creative advance into novelty
- **Organisms**: Self-organizing process systems

## Summary

Whitehead's concrescence is the process by which reality creates itself moment by moment. Each actual occasion:
1. Prehends (grasps) its entire past world
2. Integrates this data with novel possibilities
3. Creates a unique synthesis guided by subjective aim
4. Perishes, becoming objective datum for future occasions

This replaces static substance with dynamic becoming, external relations with internal ones, and passive matter with active experience. For ARR-COC, this provides the metaphysical foundation for understanding VLMs as systems that don't just process information but actively REALIZE RELEVANCE--selecting from the many what matters for the one task at hand, creating novel understanding that becomes data for future interactions.

The formula "the many become one and are increased by one" is the heartbeat of both Whitehead's cosmos and relevance-realizing AI systems.
