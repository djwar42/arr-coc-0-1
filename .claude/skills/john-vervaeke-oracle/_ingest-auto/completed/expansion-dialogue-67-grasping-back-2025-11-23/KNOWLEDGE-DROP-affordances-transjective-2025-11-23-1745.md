## Key Philosophical Insights

### The Core Insight
> "An affordance is neither an objective property nor a subjective property; or it is both if you like. An affordance cuts across the dichotomy of subjective-objective and helps us to understand its inadequacy." - Gibson (1979)

### The Deepest Connection
Affordances just ARE relevance realizations made concrete in the perceptual encounter with the environment. What makes something relevant (bears on action, fits capacities, matters for goals) is exactly what an affordance is (opportunity for action relative to capacities).

### For the Meaning Crisis
The meaning crisis is, at its core, a crisis of affordance perception - a loss of the ability to see the world as transjectively meaningful. Recovery requires:
- Recovering transjective ontology
- Recovering ecological perception
- Re-establishing agent-arena coupling
- Developing practices that enhance affordance perception
