## Key Concepts Documented

### Core Concept: The Specious Present
- Present is "no knife-edge, but a saddle-back" with temporal breadth
- Duration of ~0.5 to 3 seconds depending on measurement
- Has "bow and stern" - rearward and forward-looking components
- Term borrowed from E.R. Clay, developed extensively by James

### The Saddleback Metaphor
- We "sit perched" on time looking in two directions
- Nucleus: ~12 seconds that have just elapsed
- Fringe: Vaguely vanishing backward and forward
- Overlap model: A B C D E F G -> B C D E F G H

### Thick Temporal Experience
- We perceive duration, not instants
- The mathematical instant is an abstraction never experienced
- Duration is the primary unit of temporal perception
- Synthesis is primary - duration is given, not constructed

### James' Key Insights
- "Succession in thought is not thought of succession"
- We have no sense for empty time - change is the condition
- Neurophysiological basis: overlapping brain processes at different phases
- Variations: filled vs. empty time, age effects, boredom/engagement
