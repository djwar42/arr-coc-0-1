## Early Life

**Born**: March 10, 1963, Long Beach, New York

**Childhood**:
- Jewish family on Long Island
- Early interest in magic (performed as "Ricky the Amazing")
- Discovered punk rock as teenager
- Attended hardcore punk shows in NYC

**Musical Foundation**:
- Guitar player in punk bands
- Hose (hardcore band, 1981-1983)
- Learned by doing, not formal training

> "Punk taught me that you don't need to know how to play to make music. You just need something to say."

---

## Def Jam Records (1984-1988)

### The Beginning

Founded Def Jam from his NYU dorm room in 1984:
- $5,000 from parents
- Weinstein Hall, room 712
- Partner: Russell Simmons
- Distribution deal with Columbia

### Historic Releases

**Early Def Jam Hits**:
- T La Rock & Jazzy Jay - "It's Yours" (1984)
- LL Cool J - *Radio* (1985)
- Beastie Boys - *Licensed to Ill* (1986)
- Public Enemy - *Yo! Bum Rush the Show* (1987)

### Cultural Impact

Def Jam legitimized hip-hop:
- First hip-hop #1 album (Licensed to Ill)
- Created template for rap production
- Integrated hip-hop with rock culture
- Signed future legends

### Departure

Left Def Jam in 1988:
- Creative differences with Simmons
- Wanted to explore beyond hip-hop
- Moved to Los Angeles
- Founded new label

---

## American Recordings (1988-Present)

### New Direction

Founded American Recordings in LA:
- Focus on singer-songwriters
- Return to roots music
- More intimate productions
- Artist-first philosophy

### Name Evolution:
- Originally "Def American Recordings"
- Dropped "Def" in 1993
- Became simply "American Recordings"

### Key Signings:
- The Black Crowes
- Johnny Cash
- Tom Petty
- Slayer
- Danzig

## Career Highlights by Decade

### 1980s: Hip-Hop Pioneer
- Co-founded Def Jam
- Produced LL Cool J, Beastie Boys, Run-DMC
- Helped create hip-hop as mainstream force
- "Walk This Way" changed music history

### 1990s: Genre Expansion
- Founded American Recordings
- Johnny Cash - *American Recordings* series
- Red Hot Chili Peppers partnership begins
- Tom Petty - *Wildflowers*

### 2000s: Legacy Building
- Continued RHCP albums
- Johnny Cash's final recordings
- Metallica - *Death Magnetic*
- Neil Diamond revival

### 2010s: Elder Statesman
- Kanye West - *Yeezus*
- Adele - *21*, *25*
- Black Sabbath reunion
- Became Co-President of Columbia Records (2007-2012)

### 2020s: New Chapter
- *The Creative Act* published (2023)
- Continued production work
- Podcast appearances
- Expanded influence beyond music

## The Rubin Method

### Core Elements:
1. **Strip to essence** - Remove everything unnecessary
2. **Serve the song** - Not the artist's ego, not your vision
3. **Create safety** - Artists need freedom to be vulnerable
4. **Be honest** - Direct feedback with kindness
5. **Be patient** - Great work takes time
6. **Trust the artist** - They know their vision

### What He Doesn't Do:
- Play instruments
- Operate recording equipment
- Write songs for artists
- Impose a "sound"

### What He Does:
- Listen deeply
- Ask great questions
- Provide honest feedback
- Protect magic moments
- Hold the vision
